## The North Star

The Lab will be used with a step-by-step approach to level up my understanding of the universe, using **Data Science** to gain insights from phenomena and **Python** scripts to simulate events. This helps improve both my programming skills and thought process, enabling me to visualize these aforementioned events.

The **North Star** of The Lab at this moment is a **Schwarzschild Black Hole** simulation using Python. This is the simplest type of black hole one may find, as it is stationary (it doesn't rotate), perfectly symmetrical, and doesn't have an electric charge. It is the perfect starting point to understand this magnificent phenomenon under ideal circumstances.

To get to this point, I won't be skipping the basics: *Newtonian Physics* are the first steps, until I eventually build my understanding to reach *General Relativity* and *Quantum Mechanics*.

I'll concisely explain the historical context behind discoveries and events, along with the theory and how to break down the mathematical equations into simpler concepts. Of course, the code will be available as well, with respective comments to explain the **why** of each computational decision.

**Mathematics** and **Physics** might be daunting, but I'll make sure to explain complex formulas, such as these:

$$\frac{d^2x^\mu}{d\tau^2} + \Gamma^{\mu}_{\alpha\beta}\frac{dx^\alpha}{d\tau}\frac{dx^\beta}{d\tau} = 0$$
> Geodesic Equation

$$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$ 
> Einstein Field Equation

I will break these down in an understandable way, even if you're not familiar with the language.

So, to wrap it up, know that I'm thankful for your presence during my path towards excellence, in the hopes of inspiring and being helpful to those who are curious. I'll be thrilled to know about your journey as well, so make sure to get in touch!

All the best.
